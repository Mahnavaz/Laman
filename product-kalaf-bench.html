Kalaf Bench

Kalaf, as the central core in Laman's urban furniture system, offers an intelligent integration of nature and interactive seating. The planter structure is made of galvanized steel sheet, ensuring durability and technical resistance against moisture and corrosion. The L-shaped seats are covered with recycled railway sleeper wood to create a beautiful connection between the authenticity and warmth of wood with the industrial strength of metal. The key feature of this system is its high connection flexibility; the seating surfaces can be attached to the flowerbox at different angles, allowing designers to create a focused, dynamic, and dedicated space for pause and interaction.

Green Core of Seating; Intelligent Integration of Nature and Urban Interaction

Kalaf Bench, as one of the key products in Laman's urban furniture system, is designed for spaces where the city needs a real 'pause point'; a point that can simultaneously strengthen greenery, organize seating, and naturally shape social interaction. Kalaf is not merely a bench, but a central core for organizing space; a place where nature and human presence meet in a unified structure.

Space-Focused Design; From Individual Seating to Forming a Small Square

The design logic of Kalaf is based on creating focus in public space. A central flowerbox as the main element combines with seats placed around it, creating a readable and defined space for pause, conversation, and collective experience. This approach enables urban greening without requiring ground planting; a suitable solution for sites with implementation limitations or the need for more precise space control.

Seats can be positioned on different sides of the flowerbox; from one-sided and two-sided combinations to L-shaped, three-sided, and four-sided arrangements. This flexibility allows the designer to define their desired spatial arrangement according to site conditions, movement flow, and type of use; from a compact seat to a form of 'small square' at a local scale.

Green Barrier; Defining Territory and Organizing Space

The flowerbox barrier in Kalaf Bench has a role beyond plant maintenance. This barrier makes the seating point more readable, enclosed, and identity-rich compared to a simple bench, and can also act as a tool for soft space separation. In urban scenarios, Kalaf is a suitable option for placement at the street edge; in a way that the flowerbox on the street side creates a green and safe barrier, and the sidewalk side transforms into a calm and defined space for citizens' seating and pause.

Material and Construction; Industrial Durability with Wood Warmth

The flowerbox body is made of galvanized steel sheet to have reliable technical durability against moisture, corrosion, and harsh outdoor conditions. The seats are covered with recycled railway sleeper wood; a choice that is both faithful to environmental sustainability and brings the warmth of natural wood texture into the urban experience. This controlled contrast between industrial metal and authentic wood reflects Laman's design language: simple, readable, and enduring.
